How Matrix Multiplication Works

To multiply two matrices A (m×n) and B (n×r), the number of columns in A must equal the number of rows in B.

The element at position (i,j) in the result matrix is calculated by taking the dot product of the i-th row of A and the j-th column of B.

In this example: A is 4×3 and B is 3×2, so the result will be a 4×2 matrix.

Formula: C[i,j] = Σ (A[i,k] × B[k,j]) for k = 1 to n
