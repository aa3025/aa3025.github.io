Module Description

This module provides an introduction to discrete mathematics, a broad subject with myriad applications to computer science. Mastering these topics prepares you for advanced computing modules and technical career challenges.

Phase 1: Foundations

Symbolic logic, set theory, and axiomatic proof—vital for demonstrating software and hardware reliability.

Phase 2: Applications

Number theory (RSA Cryptography), Graph theory (Networks), and Analysis of Algorithms.

How to Succeed

"Math is a skill... not a trait that some people have and others don't."

Engage with uncertainty. If you struggle, it just means there's a specific topic you haven't mastered yet.

Communication is key. Explaining concepts to colleagues is the best way to master new material.

Weekly Topics

Week 1-3

Logic & Proofs

Sets, Symbolic Logic, Inference Laws, Axiomatic Proof, and Induction.

Week 4-5

Functions & Combinatorics

Cardinality and counting principles.

Week 6-7

Number Theory & RSA

Modular arithmetic and its application in cryptography.

Week 8-10

Graphs & Algorithms

Traversal, Searching, Heapsort, and Huffman Encoding.

Week 11-12

Complexity & Error Correction

Humming Codes, Bin Packing, and final Revision.

Assessment Breakdown

I. Core Assignment

Pass / Fail
  • Online test (Inspera) available from Week 6.
  • Up to 3 attempts allowed.
  • 25 questions in 1.5 hours per attempt.
  • Pass Mark: 20/25.

II. Applied Core Assignment (Portfolio)

100% Mark
  • Given out in Week 9, Deadline in Week 12.
  • Single-attempt individual project/portfolio.
  • No time limit (can pause/resume).
  • Determines your final module grade.
